We collect several different types of information for various purposes to provide and improve our Service to you.

Types of Data Collected

While using our Service, we may ask you to provide us with certain personally identifiable information that can be used to contact or identify you ("Personal Data"). Personally identifiable information may include, but is not limited to:

  • Email address (if user logs in or provides it for support)

Usage Data

We may also collect information that your device sends whenever you visit our Service or when you access the Service by or through a mobile device ("Usage Data").

This Usage Data may include information such as your device's Internet Protocol address (e.g. IP address), browser type, browser version, the features of our Service that you use, the time and date of your use, the time spent on those features, unique device identifiers, and other diagnostic data.

When you access the Service with a mobile device, this Usage Data may include information such as the type of mobile device you use, your mobile device unique ID, the IP address of your mobile device, your mobile operating system, the type of mobile Internet browser you use, unique device identifiers and other diagnostic data.

Data from AccessibilityService API

ShieldX utilizes Android's AccessibilityService API to perform its core content blocking functions. This service allows ShieldX to:

  • Detect URLs in browsers to evaluate against blocklists.
  • Identify specific in-app content or features (like YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels) to apply user-configured blocking rules.
  • Perform actions like navigating back or to the home screen when blocked content is detected.

We do NOT use the AccessibilityService API to:

  • Collect personal or sensitive user data that is not directly related to the blocking functionality.
  • Record audio or keystrokes.
  • Change user settings without their consent.
  • Circumvent Android privacy controls.

All data accessed via the AccessibilityService API is processed locally on your device for the sole purpose of content blocking as configured by you. We do not log or transmit this specific accessibility event data off your device for other purposes.

Data from NotificationListenerService

ShieldX may request access to the NotificationListenerService to enable features that suppress notifications from specific applications as configured by you within user-defined profiles or rules. When this feature is enabled, ShieldX can access notification content only for the purpose of identifying the source application and determining if its notifications should be suppressed according to your settings. We do not log or store the content of your notifications.
